Nothing is quite as peachy as this easy-peasy peach crumble. Who among us would ever turn down a fruit-packed, streusel-topped dessert warm from the oven? A big scoop of vanilla ice cream alongside is, of course, non-negotiable.

Different from a cobbler, which is made with a fluffy, biscuit-like topping, a peach crumble is topped with a streusel crumb made from flour, sugar, melted butter, and a little cinnamon. It’s that sweet, buttery, crisp texture that pairs so perfectly with the soft baked peaches underneath. Don't get us wrong, we love a good cobbler. But you'll find this peach crumble at the very top of our summer baking list.


Peach Crumble vs. Peach Cobbler

If asked to choose between crumble and cobbler, it's certainly hard to pick a favorite. Cobblers feature a cakey, biscuit-like topping, dolloped over fruit and baked until golden. The topping soaks up a bit of the juice from the fruit, for a tender, fruit-forward result that goes down easy.

Crumbles (and crisps) are a bit different. The same tender fruit filling is baked until bubbly, but topped with a crumbly, golden brown topping that's almost the texture of a crisp granola or cookie. This topping provides a nice textural contrast to the richness of the gooey fruit filling, and can also impart its own flavor to the mix, with possible additions of oats, brown sugar, nuts, or spices. Overall, crumbles and cobblers are both delicious in their own way, but each is unique.



Ingredients for Peach Crumble

A bubbly filling of spiced ripe peaches contrasts a crisp, buttery brown sugar crumble in this decadent summer dessert. To make a peach crumble, you'll need:

For the peach filling:

  • Sliced peaches: The star of the dessert, adding a vibrant, fresh peach flavor to the dish.

  • Fresh lemon juice: Brightens the peaches and balances the additional sugar added to the mix.

  • Vanilla extract: Adds depth.

  • Granulated sugar: Sweetens the peaches. Can be adjusted based on the sweetness of the fruit.

  • Cornstarch: Thickens the peach mixture for a spoonable filling.

  • Kosher salt: Enhances the overall flavor.

  • Ground cinnamon: Adds warmth and spice.

  • Ground nutmeg, or cardamom: Additional spice to add complexity to the filling.
